# Collision

## Role

Contact generation and broad/narrow-phase ownership.

## Data Model

This element owns contact pairs, broad-phase candidates, narrow-phase confirmation within its family boundary.
It may consume bounded lower-substrate data, bounded material lookup outputs, and bounded cross-family bridge records only through the canonical solve order.
Numeric ceilings and bridge limits are frozen by constitutional law and `STRATUMX_NUMERIC_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH.md` where shared.

## Solve Matrix

| Item | Canonical law |
|---|---|
| local solve scope | bounded regional or batch solve only |
| same-tick bridge intake | bounded by cross-family bridge law |
| publication timing | fixed by runtime phase and family solve order |
| offscreen/far posture | degradable only through simulation-tier law |

## Canonical Law

- this element may not take ownership of another family's truth surface;
- same-tick records are legal only in bounded canonical shapes;
- far or decorative work degrades before near critical work under profile law;
- hidden second-order solves outside the family boundary are illegal.

## Failure Posture

- if ceilings are threatened, optional or approximate contacts degrade before near critical contacts;
- if same-tick bridge payload threatens the family budget, excess records defer or collapse according to bridge law;
- this element may not widen runtime queues to protect its own backlog.

## Illegal Patterns

- cross-family ownership grab;
- unbounded same-tick coupling;
- far/offscreen contacts consuming near entitlement;
- hidden per-frame or per-tick secondary solve outside canonical order.
